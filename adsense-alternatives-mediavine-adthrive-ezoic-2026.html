Best AdSense Alternatives for Publishers in 2026: Mediavine, Raptive, and Ezoic Compared

We've already made the case that AdSense is usually the weakest monetization option once you have real traffic. That article covers the RPM math in detail. This one answers the question that comes right after: once you're ready to leave, which network do you actually move to? The three premium display ad networks publishers ask about most are Mediavine, Raptive (formerly AdThrive), and Ezoic, and they are not interchangeable. Each has different traffic requirements, different revenue splits, and fits a different stage of publisher growth, and the entry points have genuinely shifted over the past year as competition among these networks has intensified.

The traditional entry points still cited most consistently across the industry are Mediavine's 50,000 session monthly threshold and Raptive's 100,000 monthly pageview requirement. Treat these as the reliable baseline. That said, several networks have also rolled out lower tier onramps in the past year: Mediavine now offers "Journey," a starter program for publishers with as few as roughly 1,000 monthly sessions at a lower initial revenue share that steps up as you grow into the standard program. Ezoic dropped its traffic floor entirely through its "Access Now" program, meaning any publisher can access its AI driven optimization from day one, though its full featured monetization (premium demand, video) generally performs best once you're past 5,000 to 10,000 monthly sessions. A handful of reports also describe Raptive testing a reduced pageview threshold for select publishers. Treat that as an emerging, not yet universally confirmed, option and verify current requirements directly on each network's application page before assuming you qualify.

Ezoic is the practical entry point for publishers still under 50,000 sessions who've outgrown raw AdSense but don't yet qualify for Mediavine's standard tier. Its AI driven ad testing (Ad Tester) automatically experiments with placement and density to find the highest RPM configuration for your specific traffic, though the learning period, often 30 to 90 days, means RPMs can start lower than AdSense before optimizing upward, and revenue can fluctuate week to week as the system keeps testing. Ezoic's revenue share tends to be the most generous of the three, with publishers typically keeping around 90% of ad revenue.

Mediavine sits in the middle: a 50,000 session monthly threshold for the standard program (or the lower Journey tier below that), but historically the best regarded network for publisher support, community, and a genuinely simpler technical integration than Ezoic's more hands on optimization dashboard. Most lifestyle, food, and DIY publishers moving up from AdSense land here first, with publisher reported RPMs commonly in the $15 to $40 plus range depending on niche and season.

Raptive (the rebrand of AdThrive) has the highest bar to entry among the standard programs, and it's generally regarded as paying the strongest RPMs of the three once you qualify, particularly in finance, health, and other high CPM verticals. Some publisher case studies describe RPM jumps of 30% or more after switching over from Mediavine at scale. It's the network most publishers are trying to graduate into, not the one they start with.

What Changes Operationally When You Switch Networks

Moving off AdSense isn't a one line code swap, and underestimating the operational lift is the most common reason publishers delay a switch that would meaningfully increase their income. Expect a required site speed and Core Web Vitals review before approval, since all three premium networks reject sites with poor page speed given their heavier ad stacks make an already slow site worse. You'll also need to remove any conflicting ad code (AdSense auto ads specifically) since running two competing ad networks simultaneously creates bidding conflicts that hurt both.

Plan for a transition dip. Most publishers see a one to two week period of lower than expected RPM immediately after switching networks while the new network's demand partners calibrate to your inventory. This is normal and not a signal the switch failed. Judge performance on a 30 day average, not the first week.

Ad density and placement rules also differ meaningfully between networks. Mediavine and Raptive both manage ad placement centrally through their own scripts once installed, which means you lose some manual control over exactly where ads appear in exchange for their optimization engine handling it, and both are engineered specifically to minimize additional speed impact, since a slow site actively hurts their own revenue too. Ezoic gives publishers more granular control but requires more active monitoring to avoid over optimizing for RPM at the expense of user experience and page speed.

Beyond the Big Three: Other Networks Worth Knowing

Mediavine, Raptive, and Ezoic dominate the conversation, but they aren't the only options, and a few second tier networks fill genuine gaps. Monumetric sits between Ezoic and the premium networks, offering personal account management without Mediavine's traffic bar, typically starting around 10,000 monthly pageviews, and is worth considering for publishers who want human support earlier in their growth. SHE Media specializes in female oriented content categories like parenting, fashion, and wellness, and can outperform the generalist networks in those specific niches. For niche sites in categories the premium networks tend to avoid, adult content adjacent topics, gambling, or aggressive affiliate heavy sites, self serve networks like Adversal or Setupad offer non exclusive arrangements with lower barriers, generally at correspondingly lower RPMs.

None of these fully replace the RPM ceiling of Mediavine or Raptive once you qualify for them, but they're useful bridges, or permanent homes for content categories the big three networks simply won't accept.

Which Network Fits Which Traffic Level

Under 10,000 monthly sessions: Ezoic's Access Now program or Mediavine Journey are both viable ways to get better than AdSense rates before hitting a standard tier threshold. Between 10,000 and 50,000 sessions: Ezoic remains the standard recommendation, with Mediavine Journey as an alternative if you want that network's publisher support earlier. Between 50,000 and 100,000 sessions: Mediavine's standard program is the typical move for most content categories, and its publisher support tends to be the best fit for creators without a dedicated ad ops person. Above 100,000 pageviews, especially in high RPM niches like finance or health: apply to Raptive directly, since the RPM uplift over Mediavine at that traffic level is usually worth the stricter application process.

Whichever network you move to, don't treat it as the finish line. Revisit this comparison annually. RPM benchmarks shift as advertiser demand and privacy regulation (cookie deprecation, consent requirements) evolve, entry thresholds keep moving as the networks compete for publishers earlier in their growth, and the network that's the best fit at 60,000 sessions may not be the best fit once you cross 200,000.

Frequently Asked Questions

What traffic do I need to qualify for Mediavine or Raptive?

Mediavine's standard program requires 50,000 sessions per month, though its newer Journey tier accepts publishers with roughly 1,000+ sessions at a lower initial revenue share. Raptive (formerly AdThrive) is most commonly cited at 100,000 monthly pageviews for its standard program. Ezoic has no hard minimum traffic requirement through its Access Now program, making it the most accessible option for publishers below the other thresholds.

How much more do Mediavine and Raptive pay than AdSense?

Publishers typically report RPMs in the $15 to $40 plus range on Mediavine or Raptive versus $1 to $10 on AdSense, though the exact uplift varies enormously by niche, traffic geography, and how well the site's page speed and ad density are optimized post switch.

Will switching ad networks hurt my traffic or SEO?

Switching ad networks itself doesn't directly affect SEO, but a poorly optimized ad implementation that slows page speed or hurts Core Web Vitals can. All three major networks require a site speed review before approval specifically to avoid this, and it's worth auditing Core Web Vitals again after the switch is live.
